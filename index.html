"It's Just A Flu"

How much more dangerous is COVID-19 than a normal flu?

Source: WebMD

By Daisy Williams
Published April. 7, 2020 8:00 PM
NOTE: All data is of April 7th 2020

As New York remains as an epicenter of the COVID-19 virus, more people are taking it seriously. Social distancing is being reinforced, non-essential businesses have been closed for over two weeks and schools have shut as well. But, some people still insist it is 'but a cold'. One quarter of the cases in the US are in New York. In the City about 5% of diagnosed cases has resulted in death. Between 2007-2014, on average the flu takes 2304 deaths per year. On the most extreme year, 2011, it cost 2462 people their lives - 1649 less than the COVID-19 has taken so far. The first corona virus related death was reported on March 14th 2020 according to Government Andrew Cuomo tweet.

I am saddened to report the #COVID19-related death of an 82-year-old women in NYC who had a pre-existing respiratory disease (emphysema).

I extend my condolences to her family during this challenging time.

— Andrew Cuomo (@NYGovCuomo) March 14, 2020

With 18433 cases of Influenza (Flu) and Pneumonia (J09-J18) between 2007 and 2014, in less than a month COVID-19 has racked up 22% of this total. The speed that it is increasing it is not compatible to a normal seasonal flu or pneumonia.
